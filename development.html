Why she is a personal inspiration: Joan of Arc represents the idea that despite your age and social status you have the power to change the world despite what anyone says.

Twelve Pillars: Reflection

Summaries of Chapters 4-7 and alignment to life.

Chapter 4: The Gift of Achievement

Summary: This chapter focuses on goal setting. It argues that the major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it, not just the outcome.

Life Alignment: As I aim to be an entrepreneur in 5 years, I realize the person I become during the struggle, disciplined and resilient is more valuable than the money I will make.

Chapter 5: The Gift of Time

Summary: Time management is actually self management. We must prioritize the pain of discipline over the pain of regret .

Life Alignment: Balancing my love for movies and music with my studies requires strict scheduling to ensure my creative hobbies don't overtake my professional growth.

Chapter 6: The Gift of Association

Summary: You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with. We must constantly evaluate our relationships and disassociate from negative influences.

Life Alignment: I am actively seeking mentors and peers who share my values of kindness and creativity, ensuring my circle uplifts my academic journey.

Chapter 7: The Gift of Learning

Summary: Formal education gets you a job self education makes you a fortune. Learning must be a lifelong habit, not just something for school.

Life Alignment: Even outside of my degree, I am reading books like this one to give me some unique perspective also to understand the realities of business ownership.
